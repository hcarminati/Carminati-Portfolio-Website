Speculation of the Year 2050

New Technology

The year 2050 holds an abundance of new technology for art and office materials. One of the best creations of the year 2050 is that of a new type of ink. This ink allows for coverage of a larger surface area with the use of less ink. Currently in 2021, one cartridge of ink lasts around 6 months, but this new ink will last you 2 or more years. With that, artists and offices are not needing to purchase as much material--like pens, markers, and paint-- as often as they do now.


Problems

A problem that those who work in offices, students, and artists are facing in 2021 is being able to print on the go, wherever and whenever they want. This combined with a demand for higher efficiency in 2050, people want a way where they can print whenever with minimal effort. In 2050, as most things are moved online, people are wanting the technology that is fast, easily accessible, and economic.


Importance

Considering the COVID-19 pandemic, it is important to be talking about a Portable Printer in 2021 because there are chances of lock downs in the future. If for whatever reason there happens to be another lock down, users will not be able to go out to buy ink. With the Portable Printer, they will not need to go out even if they could. For those that need to print physical pages, the portable printer allows office workers, students, and artists to do so from the comfort of wherever they are working and in any circumstances.

Will there be printers in 2050?

Yes, the reasons being:

  • Although it is hard to say whether there will be a new type of ink that I predicted, inkless printing is already around today.
  • Art comes in various forms and not all artists will go digital.
  • Technology is bond to fail, so commpanies need a way to store their information in a safe way.
  • Humans value touch and many people prefer reading physical books, for exmaple, rather than an iPad or Kindle.

What will it be like in 2050?

There will be a demand for efficiency. In other words, a demand for

  • Less energy needed to be put in
  • Things that require less effort to operate
  • Less material wasted
  • Spending less money
